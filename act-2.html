Chicken Adobo

The unofficial national dish of the Philippines

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 10 mins
üî• Cook: 35 mins
üçΩÔ∏è Serves: 6
‚≠ê Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900g) chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks work best)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 8 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3 bay leaves (laurel)
  • 1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the refrigerator for best flavor).
  2. Heat oil in a large pot or deep pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Remove chicken from marinade (reserve the marinade!) and brown the chicken pieces on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per side. Work in batches if needed.
  4. Once browned, pour the reserved marinade into the pot along with 1 cup of water.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. Remove the lid and continue cooking for 10-15 minutes to reduce the sauce to your desired thickness.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Add brown sugar if you prefer a slightly sweeter adobo, or more soy sauce for saltiness.
  8. Remove bay leaves before serving.
  9. Serve hot over steamed white rice and garnish with green onions if desired.
  10. Pour the delicious adobo sauce over the rice - that's the best part!
